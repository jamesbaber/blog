IES Dimmer Repair

IES Matrix sine wave dimmer racks and a deep dive into flicker-free stop-motion filmmaking

Background

The mini racks contain 3x 4-channel 2.5 kW isine modules for dimming lighting fixtures for theatre and film. Rather than traditional cheap leading edge or trailing edge dimming, these create a variable voltage sine wave. The individual channels can be combined to operate together for larger loads.

Unfortunately, they're getting on a bit and faults are now common. This takes the form of a red light on front panel that usually appears a few seconds after power is applied. This is paired with a short circuit error code on the processor module. There can be a rhythmic ticking noise that sounds similar to a relay.

History

On the 26th of May 2004, ETC purchased IES.

Since then, they released Matrix MkII (2006) which offers 6 channel 3kW sine modules. They are 4U tall, 44mm taller than the first generation. They are 98% efficient, up from 97%. MkII can also run in TL mode which emulates a trailing or leading edge SCR dimmer. Useful for LEDs with active phase decoding drivers.

ETC technicians who have repaired these modules previously have remarked about how it's usually the same component that fails. They didn't say which, but luckily they sometimes fail spectacularly.

ETC have now refused to provide continuing support for this legacy product, at a time where failures are beginning to become more and more frequent. With the move to LED ramping up, it would be silly to continue adding to the collection of ~50 new ETC Sensor3 racks.

Teardown

Remove locking bolt. Slide out module. Remove one whole side of module. Remove two screws on other side for channel in question.

Teardown and visual identification of failed part. mono construction MKP339 1uF 310V X2 but actually most of the other ones are different - is this a second failure of a repaired module? ~22.5mm spacing pins Original example is B32923 X2 MKP/SH by TDK - 1uF order code is B32923C3105+*** which, as a C type, is not approved for series-with-mains capacitive dropper applications, only parallel EMI filtering applications. About half the time, changing the capacitor is sufficient. Sometimes 4 diodes have overheated and sometimes caused significant PCB damage. need to work out what does the circuit do and what repairs are safe to carry out...

Other faults

1 A fuse blowing - just runs the local transformer so really doesn't make sense that it would blow. Probably fine to replace it, maybe they blow when there is inrush as you slam the module back in? Stops front panel LEDs and control board working Fuse holder loose - overheating PCB Loose crimps Squeeze them all but if they're charred, replace them as squeezing them will crack the plastic that has become brittle. Voltage stability

Why sine wave dimming? Stop-motion flicker. In the UK, mains voltage is allowed to vary by +10% & -6%. The allowable range is therefore 216.2 V to 253 V Tungsten lamps Sine wave dimming makes this simple, just construct a sine wave with a constant amplitude, slightly below mains voltage Colour temperature? Blown lamps? From the MKII specification "Sinewave dimmers shall include voltage regulation to limit the output voltage variation to 1V for every 10V supply voltage change (except for dimmers set to full)."
